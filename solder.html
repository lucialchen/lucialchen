A Gift Soldered

A sculptural piece depicting a gift using negative space. Assembled from a protoboard, a bent brass rod, and 14 pins using a soldering iron.

Reflection

The idea of making a floating gift ribbon came to me through reminiscing on my weekend of celebrating a friend's birthday. A gift is a universal shape and though I didn't want to follow the rules of realism, I wanted my sculpture to be recognizable. Thus, a magnifying glass and pliers were used for manipulating the singular brass rod into a bow and ribbon. Soldering the pins to the protoboard was the last step, which was more difficult than securing the brass rod due to the crowded spatiality of the pins, with some instances of spillage that I scooped onto the neighboring pads to avoid a solder bridge. As this was my first attempt at soldering, this piece represents the experimentation and slight mess which comes with learning something new. As I progress in this course, I'd like to continue making projects inspired by my life and the people in it.
